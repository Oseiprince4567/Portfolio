Spatio-Temporal Above-Ground Biomass Modelling & Forest Disturbances

Challenge:

Standard carbon accounting treats biomass loss as immediate — but NRW forest data showed this was wrong. Disturbance-driven AGB decline accumulates over subsequent years, meaning same-year accounting systematically underestimates actual forest losses.

Action:

Fused GEDI LiDAR, Sentinel-1 SAR, and Sentinel-2 MSI under GroupKFold spatial cross-validation. Trained Random Forest, kNN, and CNN models; selected RF (R²=0.959, RMSE=15.39 Mg ha⁻¹). Applied Linear Mixed Effects Model (LMEM) to attribute biomass loss to drought, bark beetle, and logging drivers across NRW at 100 m, 2019–2022.

Result:

Produced 4 consecutive annual wall-to-wall AGB maps validated against ESA-CCI and DLR products. LMEM confirmed drought (β=−5.88) as dominant loss driver. Delivered a live GEE dashboard for foresters and policymakers — no GIS software required.

Random ForestGEDI LiDARGEER / LMEMView Project

QGIS Plugin for Black Frame Removal in Georeferenced Imagery

Challenge:

Georeferenced historical and aerial rasters carry black/nodata borders that corrupt mosaics and spatial analyses. QGIS's built-in NoData transparency leaves residual edge noise and requires manual per-file intervention — unworkable at scale.

Action:

Built and published a PyQGIS + GDAL + NumPy plugin using raster footprint detection and edge-safe morphological operations to automatically isolate and strip black borders. Tested on real-world sites including Hegnach, Unterkirnach, Berlin Mitte, and Berlin Südkreuz historical imagery. Published live to the official QGIS Plugin Repository.

Result:

Plugin produces clean GeoTIFFs with optional embedded alpha channel and auto-reloads results into the active QGIS project — zero manual file management. Outperforms native NoData workflows on all tested imagery types.

EV Charging Stations Activity Dashboard — Hamburg SensorThingsAPI

Challenge:

Hamburg's open EV charging IoT stream (SensorThings API) had no unified analytics layer — city planners and researchers had no way to track spatial demand, station utilisation, or temporal patterns across the network.

Action:

Built a fully serverless pipeline: GitHub Actions cron job fetches new IoT observations every 6 hours → stores in Neon PostgreSQL cloud DB. Streamlit app (evhamapp.py) queries the DB and renders Folium heatmaps, color-coded station markers, Plotly time-series and radar charts for top-10 active stations, session durations, and daily utilisation. Modular codebase: ingestion (fillin.py), analytics (hamburghelpers.py), UI (evhamapp.py).

Result:

Live dashboard deployed on Streamlit Cloud showing real-time availability, connector counts, spatial intensity heatmaps, and comparative radar charts — all auto-updated every 6 hours without manual intervention.

Concrete Crack Detection Using TensorFlow/Keras & Streamlit

Challenge:

Manual concrete inspection is slow, subjective, and cannot quantify crack area for maintenance prioritisation. Existing tools lacked an accessible, deployable interface for non-experts.

Action:

Trained a lightweight CNN (TensorFlow/Keras) on 6,000 balanced samples from a 40,000-image dataset (20k cracked / 20k non-cracked, 120×120 px). Used early stopping (patience=3), stratified 70/30 split, and ImageDataGenerator for preprocessing. Deployed via Streamlit with OpenCV edge-detection overlay highlighting crack pixels in red. Model cached with @st.cache_resource for efficiency.

Result:

Live app at crackdetect-pro.streamlit.app supports batch image upload, outputs crack area in px² and % image coverage (e.g. 103,270 px² = 7.17%; 196,273 px² = 37.86%), and generates downloadable inspection reports — deployable for structural engineering and infrastructure maintenance workflows.

Urban Carbon Monoxide Monitoring & Nighttime Light Energy Analysis

Challenge:

No sub-national, multi-year spatial comparison of CO pollution and energy-use intensity existed across all 16 German states — leaving urban planners and policymakers without evidence-based tools for targeting air quality interventions.

Action:

Integrated Sentinel-5P L2 CO (mol/m²) and VIIRS Nighttime Lights radiance in GEE for 2019–2023. Applied GADM Level-1 state boundaries, excluded June–July VIIRS data to remove wildfire and sunlight interference, computed monthly averages and multi-year means, and applied 5th–95th percentile quantile stretching per state. Built interactive dropdown UI with time-series charts and GeoTIFF export to Google Drive.

Result:

Confirmed co-location of High CO and Very High nighttime lights in NRW's industrial west, Berlin, Hamburg, and Stuttgart — quantifying urban-rural gradients across all 16 states. Live GEE app deployed for public exploration; GeoTIFF exports enable downstream GIS workflows.

Building Segmentation with Deep Learning in QGIS — Deepness Plugin

Challenge:

Extracting building footprints at very-high-resolution (sub-metre) without custom training infrastructure — comparing model performance across urban (Feuersee) and rural (Konradin-Kreutzer Weg) environments at two spatial resolutions.

Action:

Deployed the RAMP pretrained Segmentor model via the Deepness QGIS Plugin. Mapped 4 input image bands to 3 model channels; processed at 256×256 px tiles at both 40 cm and 50 cm resolutions across both sites. Recorded building and background area statistics for each run.

Result:

Feuersee (urban): 77,159 m² buildings at 40 cm vs 92,119 m² at 50 cm — 50 cm captured more structures. Konradin-Kreutzer (rural): 18,588 m² at 40 cm vs 24,767 m² at 50 cm. Confirmed 50 cm + 256 px tiles as optimal configuration. Before/after swipe videos validate model performance visually.

Mannheim City Center 3D Flood Risk Simulation Using QGIS

Challenge:

Mannheim's proximity to the Rhine and Neckar rivers makes it highly flood-vulnerable, yet no accessible 3D visualisation of building-level inundation risk existed for the city center — limiting risk communication to planners and the public.

Action:

Extracted OSM building footprints via QuickOSM, processed DTM and DSM (void-filled), applied Raster Calculator flood simulation at ≤5 m elevation threshold, computed building heights via Zonal Statistics on DSM, then extruded buildings and flood polygons into interactive 3D using QGIS2ThreeJS. Slope rasters identified natural barriers like dykes.

Result:

Of 11,720 buildings in the GeoPackage, 7,935 (67.7%) fall within the ≤5 m flood zone. Delivered interactive 3D scenes and video walkthroughs showing inundation extent, with central areas less affected due to elevation planning and existing dykes.

Chlorophyll Estimation App for Lakes in Baden-Württemberg

Challenge:

Monitoring chlorophyll trends across five ecologically distinct Baden-Württemberg lakes — from the internationally studied Bodensee to small-network lakes like Illmensee — without costly field sampling or lab analysis over a 7-year window.

Action:

Built a GEE interactive app computing NDCI (Bands B5−B4 / B5+B4) from cloud-filtered Sentinel-2 imagery, masked by NDWI for water-only pixels. Applied 5th–95th percentile dynamic colour stretch. Built dropdown UI for lake and year (2018–2024) selection, annual mean NDCI time-series charts, and GeoTIFF export to Google Drive for further analysis.

Result:

Live app deployed at GEE for public use — enables limnologists, environmental managers, and students to query chlorophyll class distributions (clear: NDCI < 0.1 through very high: >0.6) and detect seasonal eutrophication signals across all five lakes, year-by-year, in any browser.

Geomarketing for IKEA Store Site Selection in Germany

Challenge:

IKEA requires 20,000–25,000 m² sites in cities of 100k+ population, with high purchasing power, good road/rail access, and low proximity to competitors (XXXLutz, Roller). Identifying genuinely underserved German cities using spatial evidence rather than intuition.

Action:

Built 50 km exclusion buffers around all existing IKEA stores to isolate underserved zones. Applied Euclidean distance reclassification for purchasing power (ESRI Living Atlas), road/rail network buffers (700 m, Deutsche Bahn + DIVA-GIS), and competitor proximity. Ran location-allocation optimisation to rank candidate plots meeting all suitability criteria simultaneously.

Result:

Selected Trier (pop. 110,674 — near rail/road nexus, high purchasing power, coordinates: 328463E 5513063N) and Ingolstadt (pop. 136,952 — Audi AG hub, high employment, coordinates: 11.4488°E 48.7750°N) as the two optimal sites, supported by evidence maps for each stage of the analysis.
